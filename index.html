About Me

I am currently a Canadian Statistical Sciences Institute (CANSSI)/ Statistical and Applied Mathematical Sciences Institute (SAMSI) postdoctoral fellow at the University of Victoria, Pacific Climate Impacts Consortium and School of Earth and Ocean Sciences.

I got my PhD in Statistics from Purdue University in Summer 2017 where I was advised by Hao Zhang. I was actively involved in the Research Network for Statistical Methods for Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (STATMOS) and the Center for Robust Decisionmaking on Climate and Energy Policy (RDCEP), where I work with Michael Stein, Elisabeth Moyer at the University of Chicago, and Doug Nychka at the National Center for Atmospheric Research.

Before coming to Purdue, I received a Master degree in Statistics at University of Akron and a Bachelors degree in Mechanical engineering at National Cheng Kung University, Taiwan. Here is a current copy of my CV.

My research interests include statistics of extremes, spatial, spatio-temporal data analysis with applications in climate, and computer experiment
